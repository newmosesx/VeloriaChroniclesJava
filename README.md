
Here is the true story of Veloria Chronicles, written as the epic narrative it was meant to be.

The continent of Veloria is bleeding. Once a proud union of seven kingdoms bound
by trade and an uneasy truce, it is now a fractured empire suffocating under the
fading rule of King Althar. It is a land where unlicensed magic is punishable by
fire, granaries are emptying, and the air smells more of ash and rebellion than
peace.

At the crumbling fortress town of Thalor’s Gate, four broken strangers are drawn
together by hunger and the promise of mercenary coin. Kaelen Duskbane is a young
but deeply traumatized former soldier of the Crown, haunted by the atrocities he
was ordered to commit at the Siege of Halewind. Having discarded his royal
falcon crest in the ashes of the city he burned, he is now a man walking the
edge of a blade. He is joined by Lyra Veylen, a compassionate but terrified
fugitive healer hiding her illegal magic; Bram Thorne, a disgraced,
heavy-drinking knight who survived the horrific Black Crusades; and Iriah Sable,
a ruthless rogue whose loyalty extends only as far as her coin purse.

Their uneasy alliance is tested immediately when they are hired by a desperate
farmer to find his missing children in the cursed woods of the Bleeding Vale. In
the deep shadows, they don't just find beasts; they find unnatural abominations.
They are forced to battle wolves with peeling flesh and a horrific golem
stitched from shadows and metal. These monsters are driven by shards of glowing
black iron hammered directly into their bodies. They manage to rescue the marked
children, but the victory brings a horrifying revelation. The iron shards are
not just weapons; they are living corruption. Kaelen claims one of the shards,
and it immediately begins whispering dark, hungry promises into his mind,
threatening to consume him.

Far in the north, a different kind of war is being waged. Mara Voss, a former
magistrate turned rebel leader, and Joric Fen, a disgraced but brilliantly
tactical garrison captain, are leading a calculated insurgency. They fight not
to conquer, but to dismantle the Crown's control over the people's livelihoods.
After executing a flawless ambush on a royal supply convoy, they uncover a
terrifying truth hidden in a captured manifest. The Crown is not falling victim
to the monsters in the woods. The Crown is forging them, systematically
distributing the cursed shards to outposts across the land to maintain their
tyrannical grip on the empire.

Meanwhile, the heart of the Empire is rotting from within. In the grand assembly
hall of the capital, the political council collapses into open hostility.
Protected by ancient rules of the House, a council member commits borderline
treason, openly screaming at the Emperor. The empire is starving, the treasury
is being looted by rebels, the cities are falling into disarray, and the sole
ruler of the lands sits in complete silence, doing absolutely nothing to stop
it.

The pieces are set. A silent Emperor letting his kingdom burn, a calculated
rebellion uncovering a monstrous conspiracy, and a band of deeply untrustworthy
mercenaries caught in the middle, led by a man slowly being corrupted by the
very weapon the Crown created.
